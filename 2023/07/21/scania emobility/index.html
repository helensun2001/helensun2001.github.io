Understanding China NEV Industry

The internship in Capgemini Invent helps me understand how the auto industry is gradually integrated with energy landscape in China. Here’s what I leveraged from this project.

1 Case Overview

The main task was to answer the following WHs:
whether, how, where, when could the client enter China’s e-truck market?

The analysis should be backed up with policy studies and market insights, and help the client understand:

  • The driving forces of electric trucks market in China.
  • How policies shape e-truck market in China.
  • How different relationships between OEMs and government departments affect the market performance.

2 Methodology learnt

2.1 Political aspect of industry analysis

PEST model is efficient in analyzing external factors of an industry. In China, the P has complex implications.

POLITICS include:

  • A pyramid structure of political documents; E.g., national strategies, industry plannings, local policies and etc.
  • The administrative structure involving central and local governments, which actively build connections with associations, companies to implement their governance objectives.

In an industry which policies and governments play a critical role, such as energy and electrification transition of auto, the market development and policy release are two inter-weaving trends.
A burst of policy documents marks the end of market exploration and fast growth.

The policy framework could serve as the signifier of technology maturity, operational risk of new market segments.
However, this could hardly be applied to rural development, a more policy and government dependent field.
