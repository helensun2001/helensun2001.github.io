Decarbonization of Beef Cattle Husbandry

This case study was done in May, 2023 for 2023 Baincup. My team was called TheEssence, taking four graduate students from Tsinghua, Fudan & ESSEC together to build up a strategic planning slide in one week.
The case was admitted into the second round of competition as one of the top 12 teams world wide.

1 Case Background

Animal husbandry pressure China’s CH4 emission control through animal enteric fermentation and manure management.

A growing cattle husbandry industry produces the largest share of carbon emission among all animal subsectors.

2 Case deliverable

2.1 Industry Study

Chinese cattle husbandry businesses face the challenge of a low level of scaled production and backward carbon neutral actions.

Chinese cattle husbandry businesses and individuals are unprepared to target carbon neutral due to a lack of motivation and capability. Integrated beef processing companies should prioritize upgrading production techniques and efficiency, while independent farms should be driven to form scaled production through regional businesses merging.

2.2 Problem defining

We selected the Nature Conservancy(TNC) as our NPO client, considering its resources and experiences in land protection projects.

We defined TNC’s role as consultant and coordinator in decarbonizing beef cattle industry.
Then we mapped out a two-stage strategy for TNC to:

  • first, test experiences and technology through trials;
  • second, empower the whole value chain by expanding the experiences.

2.3 Strategy Summary

Based on this comprehensive evaluation model, we selected Hengdu Group as the trial company, Henan Xinxiang and Yunnan Dali as trial regions.


Hengdu is advised to test digital husbandry devices, intelligent feeding, upgrading manure recycling system and other approaches, supported by benchmarking analysis with global best practices.

Xinxiang and Dali are advised to adopt excellent species and cooperate with local planting and manure processing enterprises to form scaled husbandry zones or towns, with the help of policy subsidies and government involvement.
