Greeting Thao from Tsinghua GBJ

本文最后更新于：2022年10月10日 上午

5 questions to know about Thao Nguyen, a new student of Tsinghua Global Business Journalism program from Vietnam.

Carrer in Hochiminh

Q1: Before you come to Tsinghua GBJ program, where were you?

Thao: I worked for CHANNEL as a marketing manager the year before. Before that I worked for Unilever and then moved to the luxury brand. I was based in Hochiminh city in Vietnam, while I completed my undergraduate school in London, UK.

Why Tsinghua GBJ

Q2: How did you made the decision to come to Tsinghua?

Thao: I learned about the syllabus, courses, and culture, and found the university is well-suited for my goal. There is a psychological center that I would like to explore more. The humanity of this university and the activeness of Tsinghua students on social media attracted me.
Then I found GBJ program. There are courses focusing on mass media communication in modern China. For me, China is a nation with a diversified culture which definitely worth exploring.

Refresh to re-start

Q3: What does going back to campus means to you, given that you've had years of working experiences?

Thao: My attempt to come to Tsinghua is to refresh my career life. I’m not scoring high. Before looking at programs in Tsinghua, I was in the application process for MBA of Singapore, when I did a self-reflection and questioned whether MBA was what I wanted.

My new plan for career is to work for my own start-up, which would be a coffee chain store. My grandparents own a coffee farm in Vietnam, and I myself have rich experiences and knowledge in producing and recognizing coffee. In my plan, profits will be donated to charities that help with health problems in the young generation.

Journalism? Not a must

Q4: What do you think about journalism?

Thao: I was thinking of choosing a master’s program in communication, but I never thought about taking journalism as a career. Instead, I leverage journalism as means to support my campaign in the future. This will better my skill in writing, and help me maintain partnership for my brand.

Interest driven by Covid…

Q5: Why you concerns about mental health so much?

Thao: I have friends doing business in hotels and restaurants who faced great pressure under Covid. Though they’re restarting the business now, there was a very hard time for them, when they even got into depression. I think mental health is not only the top priority but also a fashion.


Thao is coming back to Tsinghua in the comming week. Also professors teaching in the GBJ program are planning to return to Beijing after two years of working remotely.
